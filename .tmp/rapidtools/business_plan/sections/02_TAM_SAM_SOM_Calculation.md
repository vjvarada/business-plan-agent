## Top-Down vs Bottom-Up Reconciliation

### Dual Methodology Validation

We validate our SAM estimates using two independent approaches:

| Metric                 | Top-Down                                          | Bottom-Up                                    | Reconciliation                                                      |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Software SAM**       | $336M (regional CAD × addressability × tooling %) | $9M (50 major plants × $180K)                | Bottom-up = 2.7% of top-down (50 plants = 0.1% of 50K+ companies) ✓ |
| **Enterprise Segment** | $14.7M (295 customers × $50K ARPU)                | $9-18M (50 plants × $180-360K)               | Directionally consistent ✓                                          |
| **Services SAM**       | $1.74B (650K companies × $1-15K tier-dependent)   | $1.77B ($590B manufacturing × 0.3% services) | Cross-validates within 2% ✓                                         |

### Bottom-Up vs Top-Down Methodology

**Top-Down ($336M Software SAM)** includes:

- **50K+ addressable manufacturing companies** across India + SE Asia
- **Multiple verticals:** Automotive (45%), Electronics (25%), Industrial Machinery (20%), Aerospace (10%)
- **All company sizes:** SMB (70%), Mid-market (25%), Enterprise (5%)

**Bottom-Up ($9M from 50 plants)** covers ONLY:

- **50 major automotive/two-wheeler OEM plants** (Honda, Toyota, TVS, Bajaj, Maruti, etc.)
- **Single vertical:** Automotive only
- **Single segment:** Enterprise only

**The ratio makes sense:**

- 50 plants ÷ 50,000+ addressable companies = **0.1% of market**
- $9M ÷ $336M = **2.7% of Software SAM**
- Enterprise segment with higher ARPU justifies the 27× ratio vs. company count

### Plant-Level Validation Details

Based on first-hand plant visits (see Market Drivers document for methodology):

**Fixture Inventory Estimates:**

- Honda Kolar: 2,000-4,000 fixtures (2.4M units/year, 10-15 assembly lines × 40-50 stations × 1-2 fixtures)
- Toyota Bidadi: 3,000-6,000 fixtures (342K vehicles/year, more complex 4-wheeler)
- TVS Hosur: 3,000-5,000 fixtures (4-5.5M units/year, 28 assembly lines globally)

**Industry Benchmark:** Two-wheeler assembly lines have 30-50 workstations per line, each requiring 1-3 fixtures [[ref: Honda Gujarat data, Autocar Pro]]

**Per-Plant Addressable:**

- 2,000-4,000 fixtures × 15% annual replacement = 300-600 new designs/year
- At $300-600 design cost savings per fixture = **$90K-360K/year per major plant**

**Note:** CAC values reflect conservative assumptions including pilot costs, travel, and multi-stakeholder sales cycles. Expect 2-3× increase as we expand to US/Europe markets.

### Understanding the ARPU Decline: A Deliberate "Land Enterprise, Scale SMB" Strategy

**Why ARPU Drops 70% from Y1 ($62,500) to Y8 ($18,909):**

This is **intentional and healthy**—it reflects the classic SaaS "land with enterprise, scale with SMB" motion:

| Phase          | Years | Customer Focus                         | Avg ARPU  | Why This Makes Sense                                                     |

| **Scale**      | Y5-Y8 | SMB-dominant (70% of customers)        | $19K-$55K | PLG motion kicks in; lower ARPU but much lower CAC ($2,500 vs $35,000)   |

**Why Investors Should View This Positively:**

1. **Unit Economics Improve:** SMB LTV:CAC (14.5:1) and Enterprise (11.8:1) are both excellent because CAC drops faster than ARPU
2. **Revenue Quality:** Lower ARPU from many customers > higher ARPU from few (reduces concentration risk)
3. **Market Expansion:** SMB is 70% of our TAM—ignoring it would cap growth at ~$30M ARR
4. **Bessemer Pattern:** Datadog, HubSpot, and Atlassian all followed this trajectory (enterprise validation → PLG scale)

**The Math:**

**Key Insight:** The "declining ARPU" narrative is actually a **scaling success story**—we're democratizing tooling automation beyond just enterprise customers while maintaining healthy unit economics (12-18:1 LTV:CAC) across all segments with conservative assumptions.
